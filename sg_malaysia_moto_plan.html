03City Guides — Stay · Eat · Do

All accoms retiered to the $50–80/night band. Rates are estimates — holiday-period pricing may push toward the top of range; book early.

Melaka

NIGHT 1 · DEC 24 · CHECKPOINT 1
Stay — ~$50–65/nt
Jonker Boutique Hotel — restored triple-shophouse directly on Jonker Street, advertises free private parking (confirm motorcycle access). Backup: Swiss Hotel Heritage Boutique, similar band, parking at rear.
Eat
Chicken rice balls (Hoe Kee / Chung Wah), Nyonya laksa, cendol at Jonker 88, satay celup if you're game.
Do
Evening only: Dutch Square & Christ Church at golden hour, St Paul's Hill sunset, riverside walk. One night = one tight loop, all walkable from the hotel.

Kuala Lumpur

NIGHTS 2–3 · DEC 25–26 · REST STOP
Stay — ~$60–75/nt
Bintang Collectionz — restored 95-year-old pre-colonial facade on Jalan Bukit Bintang, Peranakan-tiled rooms, Golden Triangle basecamp.
Eat
Jalan Alor hawker strip (2 min walk), Hokkien mee at night stalls, banana leaf rice in Brickfields if you venture.
Do
Day 3: Batu Caves early AM (short ride or Grab), KLCC park + Petronas view PM, Petaling Street market evening. Clustered: caves are the only leg outside the Bukit Bintang–KLCC pocket.

Cameron Highlands

NIGHTS 4–5 · DEC 27–28 · REST STOP
Stay — ~$55–70/nt
Silvan Garden, Tanah Rata — garden-set boutique with rooftop lounge; homely, modern rooms, walkable to town eateries. (Lakehouse dropped: ~2× your budget ceiling.)
Eat
Steamboat dinner (a Camerons ritual in the cool air), scones + BOH tea at Sungai Palas visitor centre, morning roadside nasi lemak stalls.
Do
BOH Sungai Palas plantation + factory tour (free/cheap), Mossy Forest boardwalk, strawberry & honey farms if you want them. Trail closures possible in wet season — ask at check-in.

Ipoh — Turnaround

NIGHTS 6–8 · DEC 29–31 · REST + SERVICE + NYE
Stay — ~$56/nt
Sarang Paloh Heritage Stay — restored 1920s former OCBC bank building on Jalan Sultan Iskandar, on the heritage trail. Parking exists but is quirky (one-way tunnel access) — confirm motorcycle arrangement when booking.
Eat
Lou Wong beansprout chicken, Sin Yoon Loong white coffee, Thean Chun kopitiam (egg custard), Aun Kheng Lim salted chicken to pack for the ride.
Do
Old Town heritage walk + Concubine Lane, Kek Lok Tong & Perak Tong cave temples, Kellie's Castle (~30min south, optional Day 8 AM). NYE: low-key in town — you ride the next morning.

Seremban

NIGHT 9 · JAN 1 · RECOVERY ONLY
Stay — ~$35–45/nt
Palm Seremban Hotel — central 3-star, deliberately below budget ceiling. This is a bed, a shower, and secure parking, not a destination.
Eat
Seremban siew pau (baked pork buns — the town's one famous export), beef noodles at the food centre. Early dinner.
Do
Nothing planned. That's the point. Lights out early; the last 300km gets ridden fresh.

Warm mid-layer + waterproof glovesCamerons runs 15–20°C and wet; the descent is cold at speed.
Fuel rule: LIKELY foreign-registered vehicles are barred from subsidized RON95 in Malaysia — pump RON97 only. The CB350 is happy on either; your wallet takes a small hit, a RON95 fine takes a bigger one. Tank range ~350km+ means each leg is doable on one fill, but top up at half-tank in the highlands — stations thin out on Rte 59.
