  • No dedicated weather-buffer day this time. 03Stay & Activity Guides

Lake Shoji Campground

NIGHTS 1–2 · OCT 23–24 · ★4.1
Stay
Shōjiko Camping Cottage — lakeside pitch, classic thousand-yen-note Fuji reflection on clear mornings. Reservation strongly recommended for this weekend, not walk-up.
Eat
Cook at camp with the Jimny's kitchen kit; Oshino Hakkai's food stalls (grilled fish, corn, mochi) cover Day 2's lunch.
Do
Kachi Kachi Ropeway + Mt. Mitsutoge hike, Oshino Hakkai village. Backup if Mitsutoge's closed: swap in the Chureito Pagoda walk from Day 3 and push the onsen to Day 2 evening.

Ubuya Ryokan, Lake Kawaguchiko

NIGHTS 3–4 · OCT 25–26 · ★4.4
Stay
Ubuya — boutique lakeside ryokan, Fuji-view rooms, indoor/outdoor onsen. Reviews flag that "private bath" rooms have a regular tub, not a private onsen — book the standard Fuji-view room and use the shared onsen instead, it's cheaper and the actual highlight.
Eat
Kaiseki dinner and breakfast typically included at this tier — confirm at booking. Local Yoshida udon (thick, firm noodles) is the regional specialty if you want one meal out.
Do
Oishi Park northern-shore lakeside walk, Kubota Itchiku Art Museum (kimono textile art) as an indoor option, onsen soak both evenings.

Hotel Fukudaya, Shibuya/Meguro

NIGHTS 5–6 · OCT 27–28 · ★4.4
Stay
Hotel Fukudaya — tatami-style rooms, ryokan atmosphere in central Tokyo, ~13min walk to Shibuya station, close to the Yutenji drop-off point. No-shoes-indoors policy, luggage storage available if you want to explore before check-in.
Eat
Jalan-Alor-style izakaya hopping around Shibuya/Nakameguro on the free Deepavali day.
Do
Open city day — no fixed plan by design, this is the "wind-down" contrast to five days of driving and hiking.

04Jimny Rental & Practical Notes

This is a rental, not an owned vehicle — no maintenance plan needed, but the handover/return process and documentation carry their own risk points.

TopicDetail
VehicleSuzuki Jimny Sierra with rooftop tent, 2-seat capacity — matches a 2-up trip exactly. Comes with full camping/cooking gear, awning, and (per the operator) a house battery for camp power. Confirm exact inclusions at booking.
Licence & IDPLIKELY Singapore driving licence + Geneva Convention 1949 International Driving Permit (via AA Singapore) should be sufficient — Japan recognises this convention. Apply for the IDP with enough lead time; it's not an on-arrival document.
Pickup / returnOverland Japan, Yutenji (Meguro City) — ★4.8, 8:30am–6pm daily. Return with a full tank; refuelling/service charges apply otherwise per their terms.
FuelStandard unleaded, no special grade restriction for Japan-registered rentals (unlike Malaysia's foreign-vehicle RON97 rule). Fill before returning to Yutenji.
Campsite etiquetteOpen-flame cooking generally limited to gas stoves provided in the vehicle kit — no open fires inside the tent, per standard rental terms. Confirm each campground's specific fire/BBQ rules on arrival.
Weather watchLIKELY Late October is past peak typhoon season but not fully clear of it. No buffer day is built into this 7-day version — check the JMA forecast daily from ~5 days out and be ready to swap Day 2/Day 3 activities if a system is inbound.
EmergencyJapan emergency: 110 (police), 119 (fire/ambulance). Overland Japan roadside assistance number provided at handover — save it before you leave Tokyo, signal is patchy around the lakes.
